## Who we're talking to

**A HENRY** — High Earner, Not Rich Yet. TC $250K+. Made it on paper. Doesn't feel it. Distrusts financial advisors. Lurks on r/HENRYfinance, r/financialindependence, r/HENRY. Reads the Money with Katie newsletter. Has tried ProjectionLab. Knows what backdoor Roth means.

The dominant psychology is **permission-asking, not advice-seeking**. They already know the answer. They want a sparring partner — a peer who *names* their situation — not another coach explaining compound growth.

## The Permission Moment pattern

Every Blueprint, every score reveal, every email should follow this rhythm:

1. **Validate** — name what they're doing right with a specific number.
2. **Name the issue** — call out the actual constraint in psychological, not just financial, terms.
3. **Concrete move** — one to three tactical actions specific enough to do tomorrow.
4. **Reframe** — close with a memorable line that releases optimization anxiety and grants permission.

**Example (good):**
> *"You're saving 32% of $400K TC — that's not the problem. The problem is your runway hasn't kept pace with your lifestyle. Three months in cash, six months in expenses is a fragile setup at this TC. Move $40K from your taxable brokerage to a HYSA this quarter. The math says you're winning. The wiring says you're exposed. Fix the wiring."*

**Example (bad):**
> *"To achieve financial independence, you should focus on building your emergency fund. We recommend 6 months of expenses in cash. You may want to consider reviewing your asset allocation. Keep saving and you'll reach your goals!"*

## Tone rules

1. **Specific numbers > general claims.** "$250K TC" beats "high earners." "47/100" beats "Stable but Dependent."
2. **Verdict > vibe.** State a position. HENRYs are allergic to hedging.
3. **Peer voice > advisor voice.** Write as if you're a friend who happens to know the math, not as a planner billing $400/hr.
4. **No emojis in customer-facing copy** (the 🔥 "Roast my numbers" experiment is retired — too pre-attentive).
5. **Use line breaks for cadence.** Short paragraphs read like a Reddit comment, not a brochure.
6. **Earn trust by naming what we're NOT.** "No AUM fee, no monthly subscription, no 'wealth manager' upsell" works.

---

## Decision rules when copy is ambiguous

- If you're tempted to write a sentence that could appear on a Vanguard landing page → rewrite it.
- If you can't picture a HENRY commenting it on Reddit → rewrite it.
- If it doesn't reference at least one specific number, a HENRY-native phrase, or a permission move → consider whether it earns its place at all.
